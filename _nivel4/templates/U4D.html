Australian Aborigine art

1. Read this brief explanation of Australian Aborigine art.

Art is at the heart of Aborigine life and is basically religious in character. Central to the religious life of Aborigine are Dreamings. Dreamings record the amazing, supernatural activities of the ancestors of the Aborigine people.

These ancestors lived in a period that lasted from the birth of the universe to a time way back in the past, beyond living memory. During this period—sometimes called the Dreamtime—such supernatural beings as the Color Snakes, for example, and the Lightning Men, and the Wagilag Sisters traveled across the world, creating everything in it and laying down the laws for social and religious behavior.

The continent of Australia is crisscrossed by an intricate network of these Dreamings. Some relate to a particular place or region; other Dreamings travel over vast distances and connect the people whose land they cover. The events of the Dreamings provide the great themes of Aborigine art.
