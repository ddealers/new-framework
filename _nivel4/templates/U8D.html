Phrasal verbs connected to relationships

1. Phrasal verbs: relationships

Match these verbs to their definition:

a) get along1. to stop being friendly because of an argument
b) break up/ split up2. to gradually have a less close relationship
c) go out3. to become friends again after an argument
d) make up4. to end a relationship
e) fall out5. to have a good relationship
f) grow apart6. to spend time regularly with somebody

2. Verb pattern: get along (with)

  • Stuart gets along with almost anyone.
  • It's amazing how well we all get along.

    The phrasal verb get along has two patterns:

    singular noun + get along +with + noun
    Lucy gets alongwith her mother.
    or
    plural noun + get along
    Lucy and her motherget along.

    Other verbs that take this pattern (with with):

    break up
    go out
    make up
    split up
    fall out

    This verb also takes the pattern, but with from:

    grow apart
