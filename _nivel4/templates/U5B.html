Passive constructions

1. Forming and using the passive: informal and formal style

Passive sentences have this form: to be (in the correct tense) + past participle

Present Perfect Passive sentences have this form: have/has + been + past participle

  • Mr. Hann has been fired.
  • Things have been stolen from their house.

    Simple Past passive sentences have this form: was/were + past participle

  • Their house was broken into while they were on vacation.
  • Many objects of worth were stolen from their house.

    Passive sentences are often used in more formal contexts, for example in newspaper reports. In conversation, you often use someone or they as a subject to avoid the passive.

  • "Someone stole our video when we were on vacation!" (Mr.. Hann's words)

  • Mr. Hann's video was stolen when he was on vacation. (from a newspaper report)

  • "They told me I'd lost my job." (Mr. Hann's words)
  • Mr. Hann's was told he had lost his job. (from a newspaper report)
