See someone doing / see someone do

1. see something do vs see something doing

  • I saw the animal climb a tree.
  • I saw the animal climbing a tree.

    In the first sentence I saw the complete action from start to finish.

    In the second sentence I saw the action in progress, but not necessarily all of it.
