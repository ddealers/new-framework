1. Introducing recent events

You use the Present Perfect to introduce recent events:

In the past two weeks, their car has broken down, their dog has run away, their frozen food has melted, Graham has lost his job and his mosquito bites have become infected.

4. Recent time

With expressions of recent time, you typically use the Present Perfect.

  • I've had a lot of work lately.
  • She's been very busy this week.

    But if the time is finished, use the Simple Past:

  • I had a lot of work yesterday.
  • She was very busy last week.
